What is Safeguarding?

Safeguarding means protecting a citizen’s health, wellbeing and human rights; enabling them to live free from harm, abuse and neglect. It is an integral part of providing high-quality health care. Safeguarding children, young people and adults is a collective responsibility.

NHS England

Signs to Watch for

Abuse can be difficult to spot, but it's important to be aware of the different forms it can take. Based on care and support guidelines, there are ten types of abuse: physical, domestic, sexual, psychological, financial, modern slavery, discriminatory, institutional, neglect, and self-neglect. Each has specific signs and indicators, though no single sign confirms abuse on its own—it’s crucial to assess the broader situation. Abuse can often be criminal, and people may experience more than one type simultaneously. Recognising these signs early is key to helping those in need.

Scie.org

Action Steps

Action Steps for Reporting Abuse or Neglect

  • Identify the Concern: If you suspect that you or someone you know is being abused or neglected, identify the signs using the information above.
  • Tell Someone You Trust: Share your concerns with a trusted individual, such as:
    • A friend
    • A teacher
    • A family member
    • A social worker
    • A doctor or healthcare professional
    • A police officer
    • Any other trusted person
  • Ask for Help: Request assistance in reporting the abuse or neglect.
  • Acknowledge Emotional Difficulties: Understand that raising concerns about abuse or neglect can be distressing for everyone involved.
  • Seek Advice: If you are uncertain about the right course of action, seek professional advice or guidance.

To Report a Crime

In an emergency, contact the Police, call 999

If the person is not in immediate danger, contact the Police, call 101
